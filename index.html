About Me

Photo Credit: XL-Dragon

Welcome! I am Tongyan, a 2nd-Year PhD student researching Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) with a focus on Digital Fabrication at Purdue University. I am interested in advancing digital fabrication for assistive and interactive devices at different scales. I pursue this by creating more accessible and intelligent digital fabrication workflows (digital), while enabling programmable functionalities at the material level (physical).

Prior to my PhD, I earned my master's degree in Industrial Systems & Engineering at the University of Washington and B.Eng in Material Science & Engineering from Peking University.

Feel free to shoot me an email to connect!

Research

My research sits at the intersection of computational design and fabrication, materials science and engineering, and mechatronics, approached through a human-computer interaction (HCI) lens.

I am passionate about interdisciplinary collaboration and have successfully partnered with labs in photonics, robotics, and soft electronics. These collaborations have not only enriched my research but also provided valuable perspectives across cutting-edge domains.
